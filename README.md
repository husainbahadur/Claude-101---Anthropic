# Claude-101---Anthropic
Platform: Anthropic Academy (Skills Jar)
Cost: Free


What was the objective?

The objective of this course was to understand what Claude is, how AI and large language models actually work under the hood, how to use them effectively, and how to think about them responsibly.

What I did

I worked through the course material which covered a mix of topics, from the technical side of how AI models work, to practical skills like prompt writing, to broader questions around ethics and responsible use.

What I learned

What AI is and how large language models work
I learned that AI models like Claude are called Large Language Models (LLMs). They're trained on massive amounts of text data and learn to predict what word or response should come next based on patterns in that data. They don't "think" the way a human does, they generate responses based on statistical patterns in their training.

I also learned that LLMs have a context window, this is the amount of text the model can "see" at one time during a conversation. Everything outside of that window, the model has no memory of.

How to write effective prompts
This was one of the most practically useful parts of the course. I learned that the quality of what you get out of an AI model depends heavily on how you phrase what you put in, this is called prompt engineering.

Key things I learned about writing better prompts:


Be specific and clear about what you want, vague prompts get vague answers
Give the model context about who you are and what you're trying to achieve
Break complex tasks into steps rather than asking for everything at once
Tell the model what format you want the output in
If the first response isn't right, refine your prompt rather than just repeating the same thing


AI ethics and responsible use
This was the part of the course I expected to be fairly surface level, but it ended up going deeper than I thought. I learned about the real challenges around AI safety, things like:


Bias: Because models are trained on human-generated data, they can reflect and even amplify biases that exist in that data
Hallucination: LLMs can confidently produce information that sounds correct but is actually wrong, this is a genuine risk when using AI for research or factual tasks
Responsible use: Understanding when AI is an appropriate tool and when it isn't, and the importance of always verifying AI output rather than taking it at face value


What I took away from this

I went into this course thinking it would be quite basic given that I already use Claude regularly, but it went deeper than I expected, especially the ethics and technical sections. Understanding how the model actually works rather than just what it produces changed how I think about using it. The prompt engineering section was immediately useful, I noticed a difference in the quality of responses I was getting just from being more deliberate about how I structured my prompts.
